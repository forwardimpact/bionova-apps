# Jobs To Be Done

The Big Hires BioNova Polaris is hired for, one entry per persona-outcome pair.
This catalogue is authored by hand — Polaris ships as one product, so there is
nothing to generate. Each Big Hire below carries its Little Hire, the narrower
daily job, in the same `<job>` block. As the product grows, further Little Hires
may be tagged near the code that serves them. Discover every job with
`rg '<job '`.

<job user="Patient / Advocate" goal="Find a Relevant Trial">

## Patient / Advocate: Find a Relevant Trial

**Trigger:** A new diagnosis lands, a clinician mentions a trial might exist, and
the public registry returns pages of protocol language written for researchers.

**Big Hire:** Help me find trials relevant to my condition without reading dense
protocols. → **Polaris**

**Little Hire:** Help me gauge whether I might be eligible before I call a
coordinator. → **Polaris**

**Competes With:** ClinicalTrials.gov keyword search; asking my own doctor;
patient forums; giving up and waiting.

**Forces:**
- **Push:** Registry results are written for researchers, not patients.
- **Pull:** A plain-language shortlist of trials I could actually act on.
- **Habit:** Searching the web and trusting the first advocacy page I find.
- **Anxiety:** Getting my hopes up about a trial I will not qualify for.

**Fired When:** my condition goes into remission; or a trusted advocacy
organization launches its own matcher.

</job>

## Clinical Development Staff: Keep Listings True

**Trigger:** A trial's eligibility criteria change and the listing patients see
is suddenly stale, drawing screening calls that go nowhere.

**Big Hire:** Help me keep public trial listings matching the current protocol.
→ **Polaris**

**Little Hire:** Help me watch which trials are drawing enrollment interest.
→ **Polaris**

**Competes With:** editing the registry entry by hand; spreadsheets; emailing the
site coordinator; doing nothing until someone complains.

**Forces:**
- **Push:** Stale listings waste coordinator time on unqualified screening calls.
- **Pull:** Confidence the public listing reflects the protocol in force today.
- **Habit:** Treating the external registry as the system of record.
- **Anxiety:** An automated sync might publish a wrong criterion.

**Fired When:** the trial closes enrollment; or the sponsor mandates a single
registry of record.

</job>

## Referring Physician: Refer in the Visit

**Trigger:** A patient in clinic asks whether any trial fits them, with three
minutes left in the appointment.

**Big Hire:** Help me search trials on behalf of patients and share the details.
→ **Polaris**

**Little Hire:** Help me bookmark a trial and hand the patient something they
understand. → **Polaris**

**Competes With:** ClinicalTrials.gov; a colleague who runs trials; the patient
researching alone; not referring at all.

**Forces:**
- **Push:** No time to parse a protocol mid-visit.
- **Pull:** A shareable, plain-language summary I can trust in front of a patient.
- **Habit:** Referring only to the handful of trials I already know.
- **Anxiety:** Recommending a trial that turns out to be a poor fit.

**Fired When:** I change specialties; or the health system routes all trial
referrals through a central office.

</job>
